---
title: Build activation dashboards
description: Turn raw events into a shared activation view the whole team can understand.
---

The Activation dashboard in Pulseboard answers a narrow but high-value question: how quickly do new users reach first value, and where do they fall off before that moment?

Each chart can be filtered by plan, acquisition source, geography, lifecycle stage, and workspace health score. Saved views become shared URLs for support and growth teams.

In a docs sprint, this is where product understanding matters. We are not just formatting a help center. We are translating the product into a usable explanation.

## Choose core metrics

Start with metrics that matter for activation:

- **Time to first activation** - How long until users reach their first value moment
- **Activation rate** - Percentage of users who activate within 7 days
- **Activation by cohort** - Compare activation across signup dates

## Add breakdowns

Enhance your metrics with meaningful dimensions:

- **Plan tier** - Compare activation across pricing plans
- **Signup source** - See which channels drive better activation
- **Geography** - Identify regional differences in onboarding
- **Company size** - Understand activation patterns by account size

## Save and share views

Create reusable views for different teams:

<Steps>

1. Start with one activation event instead of a full funnel
2. Break the chart down by plan and signup cohort
3. Save a filtered view for self-serve signups versus assisted signups
4. Share the dashboard with product and support teams

</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Best practice">
  The first dashboard should answer one decision, not ten. Shared filters are often more useful than more charts.
</Callout>

## Key points

- **Focus on one question** - Don't try to answer everything at once
- **Design for your audience** - Support teams use dashboards differently than product managers
- **Iterate based on feedback** - Let team needs guide your dashboard evolution
