Why We Built an AI Receptionist for Pet Groomers

The story behind NextReach Studio — and why 47 missed calls a week is a problem worth solving.

Every weekend, Jennifer Martinez spent Sunday evenings calling back numbers she missed during the week. She runs Barks & Bubbles Pet Salon in Denver, and like most groomers, she’s hands-on with clients all day. Her phone rings while she’s mid-groom. She can’t answer. The caller books somewhere else.

This happens 12 to 16 times a week at the average salon.

That’s not just annoying — it’s a leak. If each booking is worth $60–$80, a salon is losing $3,000–$5,000 a month to unanswered calls.

We built NextReach Studio to stop that leak.

The Problem

Pet grooming is a hands-on business. You can’t stop mid-scissor cut to answer a phone. But clients expect immediate answers:

  • “Do you groom golden doodles?”
  • “How much for a full groom on a Shih Tzu?”
  • “Do you have availability this Saturday?”

If you don’t answer, they call the next salon on Google.

Why a Human Receptionist Isn’t the Answer

Hiring a receptionist costs $2,000+/month. Most salons can’t afford that, and even if they could, receptionists don’t work 24/7. The calls that matter most often come after hours — evenings, weekends, holidays.

What We Built

An AI that:

  • Answers 24/7 — never misses a call or chat
  • Knows your prices and services — trained on exactly what you offer
  • Books appointments — captures every detail and sends it to your inbox
  • Handles FAQs — breeds, hours, location, products

It goes live in 3 days. No dev team needed. One line of code on your website.

The Results

Pilot salons saw:

  • 47 fewer missed calls per week
  • 31 new bookings in 30 days
  • 4+ hours saved per week

One groomer told us: “I don’t think about it anymore. It just handles itself.”

What’s Next

We’re expanding the AI’s capabilities every month. Upcoming features include automated follow-ups, SMS integration, and real-time availability syncing.

No hype. Just a receptionist that works.
